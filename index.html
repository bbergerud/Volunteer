Volunteering in the United States

In 2017, over $400 billion dollars was donated to charities and organizations in the United States (Carrig 2018), while around 77 million individuals donated nearly 7 billion hours towards volunteering, accounting for $167 billion dollars worth of service (National Service).

Radar Chart

One limitation to using a (univariate) choropleth map is that one can only view a single variable at any given moment, making it difficult to compare the frequency of volunteer activities, or to examine the distribution of volunteer organizations that people participate in. One non-spatial visualization method that can better aid such a comparison is a radar chart.

For the radar chart below, you can select whether to plot the distribution of Volunteering activities or organizations by selecting "Activity" or "Organization" respectively. To highlight a particular state, just select it from the menu on the left. The two color options can be used for changing the colors used on the graph. The first one corresponds to the states that are not selected, while the second color option sets the color for the selected state.
